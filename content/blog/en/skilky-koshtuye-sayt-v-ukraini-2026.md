Ask ten web studios "how much does a website cost" and you'll get ten different answers — from €70 to €11,500. The reason is simple: the word "website" hides very different products, and pricing depends on a dozen factors.

In this article we break it all down: real price ranges for 2026, what's included, what hidden costs to expect, and how to avoid overpaying. All prices are in Ukrainian hryvnia (UAH) with approximate EUR equivalents at the rate of 1 EUR ≈ 44 UAH.

## What determines website pricing

Before quoting a number, any reasonable studio considers:

1. **Type of website.** A one-page business card costs dozens of times less than an online store with 5,000 products.
2. **Number of pages or screens.**
3. **Design complexity.** Template, custom design, or a unique concept with illustrations.
4. **Features.** Forms, CRM integrations, payments, user accounts, multilingual support.
5. **Content.** Are texts and photos ready, or do they need to be commissioned.
6. **Tech stack.** WordPress on a template vs custom Next.js development.
7. **Deadlines.** Rush jobs add 20–50% on top of the base price.

## Real price ranges in 2026

These are typical numbers for the Ukrainian market in April 2026, mid-tier studios, no premium markups.

### Business card site: 5,000 – 15,000 UAH (€115 – €340)

A concise page with information about you, your services, and contacts. Up to 5 sections, no user accounts, no complex functionality.

**What you get:**

- Responsive design (mobile + tablet + desktop)
- Basic SEO (meta tags, sitemap)
- Contact form
- Google Analytics setup

**Who orders it:** freelancers, doctors, lawyers, tutors, small local businesses.

**Timeline:** 5–10 working days.

### Landing page: 8,000 – 25,000 UAH (€180 – €570)

A single-page site focused on conversion — sells a specific product, service, or offer. The structure is built to lead visitors toward a target action: buy, submit a form, subscribe.

**What you get:**

- Custom design tailored to the product
- Multiple lead capture forms
- CRM or email service integration
- A/B testing (optional)
- Extended event analytics

**Who orders it:** info products, high-ticket services, single-product sales, Google Ads / Meta campaigns.

**Timeline:** 7–14 days.

### Corporate site: 15,000 – 60,000 UAH (€340 – €1,360)

A multi-page company website with About, Services, Cases/Portfolio, Team, Blog, and Contacts sections. Usually with a CMS so you can edit content yourself.

**What you get:**

- 8–20 pages
- CMS (Strapi, Sanity, Notion-as-CMS, or custom)
- Blog with categories
- Multilingual (if needed)
- Extended SEO
- CRM, email, chat integrations

**Who orders it:** mid-sized businesses, IT companies, manufacturers, B2B services.

**Timeline:** 3–6 weeks.

### E-commerce store: 25,000 – 200,000+ UAH (€570 – €4,500+)

The widest range here, because stores can have anything from 50 products to 50,000. Key components: catalog, product card, cart, checkout, payment and shipping integrations.

**Budget tier (25–50k UAH / €570–€1,140):**

- Up to 200 products
- Ready platform (WordPress + WooCommerce, Shopify, Хорошоп)
- Basic integrations — Nova Poshta, LiqPay
- Platform admin panel

**Mid tier (50–120k UAH / €1,140–€2,725):**

- Up to 2,000 products
- Custom design
- Filters, variations, reviews
- 1C, CRM, marketplace integrations
- Customer accounts

**Complex (150k+ UAH / €3,400+):**

- Large catalog + unique business logic
- Custom Next.js / Laravel build
- ERP integrations, loyalty programs
- Behavioral analytics, recommendations

**Timeline:** 4 weeks to half a year.

## 5 common client mistakes

**1. Choosing solely on price.** A 3,000 UAH site is almost always a marketplace template thrown together in an evening. It will work — but it won't sell.

**2. Not asking about ongoing support.** A site without regular updates starts "falling apart" within a year — outdated libraries, security issues, broken design.

**3. Cutting corners on content.** You can have a perfect design, but if the texts were generated by ChatGPT in 5 minutes and photos pulled from Google — there will be no conversions.

**4. No budget for promotion.** A website is a tool. Without traffic (SEO, ads, social media) it doesn't bring in clients.

**5. Building "like a competitor's site".** Blind copying loses your uniqueness and positioning.

## How to tell if a quote is fair

Ask the studio:

- How many hours of work are included? (typical: 30–80 hours for a landing)
- What stack do they use? (modern: Next.js, Astro, Vue/Nuxt, sometimes WordPress)
- Will you have access to the code / admin panel? (you should)
- Who owns the design rights? (you should)
- What guarantees do they offer after launch?

If answers are vague — it's a red flag.

## How to save money without losing quality

- **Bring your own content.** Come with ready texts, photos, brief — knocks 15–25% off the price.
- **Clear brief.** The better you understand what you want, the fewer iterations → smaller invoice.
- **MVP approach.** Launch a minimal version now, add features later as separate invoices.
- **Don't pick the cheapest studio.** Save money on promotion, not on development. A bad website costs you 10× more in lost sales.
- **Free hosting.** Vercel/Netlify cover 90% of websites — savings of 1,000–2,000 UAH per year.
